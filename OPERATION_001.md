## The Target

MoMA is the canonical arbiter of what counts as art. It decides what enters the permanent collection, what gets shown, what gets forgotten. Its walls are funded by corporations with interests that contradict the radical practices it claims to preserve. Its ticket prices exclude the publics it claims to serve. Its board is a governance exploit hiding in plain sight.

It also has a website. An API. A ticketing system. An opsec posture.

Both are valid entry points.

---

## Your Mission

Identify and document a vulnerability in MoMA's institutional architecture.

**Register A — Symbolic / Conceptual**
A structural contradiction. A power leak. A governance exploit. A curatorial blindspot. File it as a vulnerability disclosure. Write it as a ransom note. Map it as an attack surface. The form is yours.

**Register B — Technical / White Hat**
A real security or opsec finding in MoMA's public digital infrastructure. No exploitation. No harm. Submit through CICFA — we handle responsible disclosure. The act of surfacing the flaw is the work. The ransom letter we send to MoMA is the performance.

Both registers can coexist in a single submission.

---

## Submission Formats

- **Written** — Disclosure document, breach narrative, speculative exploit paper, ransom note. 1–3 pages, any style.
- **Visual / Diagram** — Attack surface map, power flow diagram, org chart annotated as exploit topology, opsec failure diagram.
- **Web artifact** — A deployable page, tool, or interface. Link to hosted version + screenshot.

---

## Reference Vulnerabilities (Register A)

For participants needing entry points:

- Corporate board composition: Goldman Sachs, Blackstone, real estate interests
- Expansion history: PS1 acquisition, global franchise model, Abu Dhabi adjacencies
- Canon formation: whose work enters the permanent collection, at what price, by whose decision
- Labor disputes: 2021 staff unionization, wage suppression during public-facing "community" framing
- Ticket pricing: $30 general admission vs. "public institution" narrative
- Press release language: what gets redacted between the curatorial statement and the catalogue essay

---

## Responsible Disclosure Protocol (Register B)

1. Submit finding to CICFA (no exploitation, findings only)
2. CICFA generates a styled ransom letter and sends it to MoMA — this is the performance
3. 30-day remediation window
4. Finding published in CICFA archive alongside the ransom letter as artifact
5. Selected for exhibition
